Pilates is a type of mind-body exercise. Mind-body exercises are forms of exercise that provide benefits on both a physical and a mental level. It was invented by German physical trainer Joseph Pilates in the early 20th century, and uses repetitive exercises to target muscles in the body

Some of the benefits of pilates include: -

  • Flatter stomach
    •
  • Stronger back
    •
  • Increased flexibility
    •
  • Excellent full body toning (as moves target more than one muscle group simultaneously)
    •
  • Improved core stability
    •
  • Relax and refresh
    •

Matwork Pilates refers to practicing pilates on a mat without a need for any other specialised equipment.

You can find a guide to the 34 original pilates exercises here

Pilates is an excellent way to improve: -

  • Strength
    •
  • Flexibility
    •
  • Muscle tone

Pilates: -

  • Can be adapted for all fitness levels
    •
  • Incorporates weight-bearing and resistance training
    •
  • Can be done with or without weights
    •
  • Teaches body awareness and controlled movement
    •
  • Promotes good posture and flexibility
    •
  • Helps you increase strength and maintain a healthy physique
    •
  • Works by teaching you to find and se the core muscles that act like a corset around your trunk. Improving strength in these muscles gives more support to the spine and torso.
    •
  • Strengthens internal muscles while toning the visible ones, resulting in better postural awareness and a more slender appearance.
    •
  • Has significant medical benefits, as detailed below.
    •
  • Is a great complement to sporting activities and training, providing balance and stablility, and the core strength and flexibility needed to avoid injury.
    •
  • Can be modified to be continued throughout pregnancy and, after your baby is born, is an excellent postnatal tool to help restore lost muscle tone, particularly pelvic floor weakness.
    •

Once you are familiar with the principles of pilates you can tailor the movements to your personal needs.

There are a number of medical benefits to pilates including: -

  • The weight-bearing and resistance training incorporated in pilates is highly recommended by physicians, especially for maintaining quality of life in older adults.
    •
  • Strengthening the core muscles can help alleviate back problems. Pilates is regularly recommended by GPs, Physiotherapists and Chiropractors for people with chronic back pain or recovering from back injuries.
