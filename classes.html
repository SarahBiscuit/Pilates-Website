As a private client you will receive: -

  • Much more individual analysis.
  • Advice on how to address postural weaknesses.

  • Supporting documentation to use at home.

Each hour-long session will start with a warm up and some basic pilates moves.

This allows me to analyse the way you move and refresh postural awareness.

This analysis allows me to develop a tailored programme, which will be followed and built upon in each session.

We can chart your progress by: -

  • Using photos.
  • Completing a weekly diary, which will also help to track lifestyle features which affet your posture and fitness levels.

Pilates is a very accessible form of exercise for beginners because: -

  • It is non-aerobic.
  • Much of it is done lying on the floor.
  • My class sizes are monitored, so there is the opportunity for close supervision of participants.

I give careful guidance, and the basic elements of the moves will be introduced over the first 6 weeks.

Please do the following: -

  • Make me aware of your state of physical fitness.
  • Update me with any changes in your state of physical fitness.
  • It is advisable to check with your GP about undertaking new forms of exercise, and you should definitely do so if you have any doubts or concerns.

Pilates develops a deeper understanding of how muscles function to mobilise the body.

Through the first few weeks of training, moves that may initially feel easy become challenging and nuanced as you learn to be more aware of how you are moving, not just what you are moving.

If you have done pilates before you will know how effective it is.

I regularly work in gyms and with advanced practitioners.

Close supervision means that your technique is maintained, and variable options within each move means that you are always challenged.

I will tailor your programme as I get to know you to ensure that you work at your optimum level.

Pilates can absolutely be continued, or started, throughout pregnancy.

Whether you want to maintain fitness throughout pregnancy, or regain your figure and rediscover your pelvic floor after your baby is born, pilates can help.
