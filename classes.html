Pilates is a very accessible form of exercise for beginners because: -

  • It is non-aerobic.
  • Much of it is done lying on the floor.
  • My class sizes are monitored, so there is the opportunity for close supervision of participants.

I give careful guidance, and the basic elements of the moves will be introduced over the first 6 weeks.

Please do the following: -

  • Make me aware of your state of physical fitness.
  • Update me with any changes in your state of physical fitness.
  • It is advisable to check with your GP about undertaking new forms of exercise, and you should definitely do so if you have any doubts or concerns.

Pilates develops a deeper understanding of how muscles function to mobilise the body.

Through the first few weeks of training, moves that may initially feel easy become challenging and nuanced as you learn to be more aware of how you are moving, not just what you are moving.

If you have done pilates before you will know how effective it is.

I regularly work in gyms and with advanced practitioners.

Close supervision means that your technique is maintained, and variable options within each move means that you are always challenged.

I will tailor your programme as I get to know you to ensure that you work at your optimum level.

Pilates can absolutely be continued, or started, throughout pregnancy.

Whether you want to maintain fitness throughout pregnancy, or regain your figure and rediscover your pelvic floor after your baby is born, pilates can help.

Before you start, you should confirm with your doctor or midwife that it is safe for you do do pilates (as there are circumstances in which it would not be advised).

Pregnant and Experienced

If you have done pilates before, your body will probably be in good shape for the challenges of pregnancy, labour and childcare.

It will usually, therefore, be safe for you to continue. However, you will need to make some changes to your programme in order to stay safe.

One-to-one sessions will help guide you through your pregnancy and the safest way that pilates moves should be adapted for you.

It may be suitable for you to attend a mixed ability class with a modified programme to suit your needs.

Please see the section below for guidance on how to introduce pilates when pregnant.

If you are pregnant, you would be advised to do the following

  1. Get medical sign-off to start the exercise.
  2. As not all moves will be suitable for someone pregnant, book a 45 minute private session, in your own home, to discuss the safest way to proceed.
  3. You can then choose to either continue your one-to-one sessions, or join a group session understanding that some moves may need to be modified.

It is generally advised that you do not start any new form of exercise within the first 12 weeks of pregnancy as a precaution, but after that pilates can help you focus on your strength, pelvic floor and posture. By building strength from within, Pilates can help you adjust to the constant changes of your body throughout pregnancy.

Pilates can improve: -

  • Pelvic floor conditioning.
  • Back strengthening.
  • Postural awareness.
  • All over body toning.
  • And much more.
