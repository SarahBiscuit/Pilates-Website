Pilates is a very accessible form of exercise for beginners because: -

  • It is non-aerobic.
  • Much of it is done lying on the floor.
  • My class sizes are small so you will get a lot of individual support

I give careful guidance, and the basic elements of the moves will be introduced over the first 6 weeks.

We will start with a guided programme of the basic moves.

If you have done pilates before you will know how much you can gain from it.

I am very experienced and am alble to support you to a high level.

I will tailor your programme to make sure that you are always challenged.

Pilates can absolutely be continued, or started, throughout pregnancy.

Whether you want to maintain fitness throughout pregnancy, or recover and regain fitness post-partum, pilates can help.

Before you start, you should confirm with your doctor or midwife that it is safe for you to start doing pilates.

Pregnant and Experienced

If you have done pilates before, it will usually be safe for you to continue, but you will need to make some changes to your programme in order to stay safe.

One-to-one sessions will help guide you through your pregnancy and how to adapt pilates moves for your safety.

It may be suitable for you to attend a mixed ability class but the programme will need to be modified for you.

Please see the section below for guidance on how to introduce pilates when pregnant.

If you are pregnant, you would be advised to do the following

  1. Get medical sign-off.
  2. As not all moves will be suitable for someone pregnant - contact me to discuss which moves are safe.
  3. Choose whether to attend one-to-one or join a group session (where you will need to modify some moves).

Pilates can improve: -

  • Pelvic floor conditioning.
  • Back strengthening.
  • Postural awareness.
  • All over body toning.
  • And much more.

Why not organise a small group session with some other pregnant friends.
