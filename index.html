Pilates is a type of mind-body exercise. Mind-body exercises are forms of exercise that provide benefits on both a physical and a mental level. It was invented by German physical trainer Joseph Pilates in the early 20th century, and uses repetitive exercises to target muscles in the body

Some of the benefits of pilates include: -

  • Flatter stomach
  • Stronger back
  • Increased flexibility
  • Excellent full body toning (as moves target more than one muscle group simultaneously)
  • Improved core stability
  • Relax and refresh

Matwork Pilates refers to practicing pilates on a mat without a need for any other specialised equipment.

PUT INFO HERE FROM ORIGINAL WEBSITE ON HOW PILATES WORKS Pilates is an excellent way to improve strength, flexibility and muscle tone, and can be adapted for all fitness levels. It incorporates wieght-bearing and resistance training, and can be done with or without weights.

It teaches body awareness, and controlled movement, as well as promoting good posture and flexibility. Professional athletes, dancers, celebrities and supermodels have used it for decades to increase strength and maintain a healthy physique.

It works by teaching you to find and use the core muscles that act like a corset around your trunk. Improving strength in these muscles gives more support to the spine and torso. Pilates strengthens internal muscles while toning the visible ones, resulting in better postural awareness and a more slender appearance.

Pilates has significant medical benefits, as detailed below, and is a great complement to sporting activities and training, providing balance and stability, in addition to the core strength and flexibility needed to avoid injury.

Pilates can be modified to be continued throughout pregnancy. After your baby is born, it is an excellent postnatal tool to help restore lost muscle tone, particularly pelvic floor weakness.

Once you are familiar with the principles of pilates you can tailor the movements to your personal needs.
